# Live Demo Script

## Goal

Show HealthTwin as a decision system for healthcare operators and capital stakeholders, not as a generic AI demo.

The audience should leave with three conclusions:

1. this solves a real healthcare operating problem
2. this produces artifacts leadership teams can act on
3. this is differentiated because it links operator and capital decisions in one workflow

## Demo Length

Recommended live version: 4 minutes  
Recommended judged version: 2.5 to 3 minutes

## Demo Setup

Before presenting:

1. run `npm run dev`
2. open [http://localhost:3200](http://localhost:3200)
3. start on the `Projects` screen
4. use `Import MetroCare Demo`

The bundled MetroCare flow is deterministic, so it is safe for live presentation.

## Opening Talk Track

Use this wording or something close to it:

> HealthTwin is a healthcare decision workspace for provider CFOs, strategy teams, lenders, and investors.  
> When reimbursement pressure hits, most teams have the same problem: the data is fragmented, the stakeholders are misaligned, and nobody has a fast way to see what breaks first.  
> HealthTwin turns a messy evidence pack into a decision-ready operating and capital response in one workflow.

## Step-by-Step Demo

### 1. Projects

Say:

> I’ll start with a bundled reimbursement-stress case for MetroCare, a regional provider system.

Action:

- click `Import MetroCare Demo`

### 2. Review Assumptions

Say:

> Instead of starting with a chatbot, the product starts by structuring the operating frame.  
> Here we see the assumptions, the KPI baseline, and the grounded facts the run will use.

What to point out:

- reimbursement cut assumption
- denial pressure
- labor pressure
- KPI baseline
- evidence-backed facts

### 3. Inspect Stakeholders

Say:

> The next problem in healthcare is not just forecasting. It’s stakeholder conflict.  
> This view shows who is under pressure, what they optimize for, and who reacts first.

What to point out:

- provider operations
- payer
- regulator
- lender
- investor
- patient advocate

### 4. Replay Impacts

Say:

> The product then compares base, downside, and severe paths over 30, 90, and 180 days.  
> The important part is not just that margin worsens, but how access, liquidity, and covenant pressure move together.

What to point out:

- KPI deltas
- risk heatmap
- event timeline
- action scenarios

### 5. Read Memo

Say:

> This is where the product becomes useful in the real world.  
> One run produces two synchronized outputs: an operator brief for the health system and a capital memo for lenders or investors.

What to point out:

- same run
- two decision audiences
- cited outputs
- recommended actions

### 6. Compare Actions

Say:

> Finally, leadership can pressure-test next steps instead of just reading a static summary.

What to point out:

- follow-up question flow
- mitigation comparison
- confidence / blind spots

## Closing Talk Track

> Most tools either summarize documents, show dashboards, or simulate generic agent behavior.  
> HealthTwin is different because it connects evidence, stakeholders, operating pressure, financing implications, and board-ready outputs in a single healthcare-native workflow.

## Positioning Line

Use this as the headline:

> The first integrated decision workspace built specifically for provider operating stress and capital response.

That is the right way to frame it. Do not claim absolute market exclusivity unless you have done formal market diligence.
